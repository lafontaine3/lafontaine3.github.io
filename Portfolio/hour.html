How Do Drones Work?

The way a quadcopter works, is the engine makes motors turn, which cause propellers to turn, which makes the drone fly. The drone turns by having some propellers slow down, and that causes it to turn. There are many parts that make the drones work. These are in the table below. Another important thing about speed, is how airspeed works. I first must explain groundspeed. Your groundspeed is the speed you go on the ground. Like if you are walking 7 miles per hour, you are going 7 miles per hour no matter what kind of wind. Airspeed is groundspeed + thrust or – tailwind (a=g+w). Therefore, if you were a bird and you were flying 7 miles per hour, and there was a 3 MPH tailwind, you will be going 4 miles per hour. You also need to factor in the four forces of flight. Thrust, lift, drag, and weight. If you use a quadcopter, you can let your aircraft float in the air. That was how a drone works in a nutshell.



What are the parts of a drone?

The FrameHolds everything together
The motorsTurn the propellers
The BatteryGives the moters power
The PropellersGives the drone lift
The ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)Sets the speed of the motor
The TransmitterControls what direction it goes
